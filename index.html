Ph.D. Research

My main research interests focus on developing medical robots, educational robots, collaborative robots and using technologies like Virtual Reality (VR), Augmented Reality (AR), Artificial Intelligence (AI), and Machine Learning (ML) to solve real world problems through robotics. My Ph.D. research was on Frature-directed steerable needles (compliant needles that can be steered to a target location) where the following objectives were met:

  • Obtained an accuracy of 98% in predicting the motion of Stylet-and-Tube and Waterjet Steerable Needles by proposing and testing kinematic models based on Screw Theory and matching them with experiments
  • Improved the Radius-of-Curvature, Steerabilty and buckling issue of traditional Steerable Needles by designing and implementing a Waterjet Needle Steering System (mechanical design in SolidWorks, control software in Python and C++
  • Reduced the error in predicting the cut-depth of the waterjet in soft tissue to less than 2% by developing a finite element model of the tissue and the needle in ANSYS as well as a closed-form mathematical model based on the mechanical properties of the soft tissue (constitutive response and fracture toughness) and the waterjet needle (diameter and velocity)
  • Accurately measured the distance and curvature as well as reconstructed the needle path from the images by developing an Image Processing Software in MATLAB
  • Decreased the targeting error of the Waterjet Steerable Needle to less than 2 mm by developing an optimal path planner based on Genetic Algorithm
  • Successfully controlled the Waterjet Steerable Needle to a target by developing a Vision-based control scheme using OpenCV and MATLAB
  • Accurately measured the low-strain-rate and high-strain-rate properties of SEBS soft tissue simulants by fitting Ogden model to the results of the Static Compression and Split-Hopkinson Pressure Bar (SHPB) tests
My Ph.D. research at Washington State University was partially funded by National Science Foundation (NSF).

Research and Projects during my Master's in Mechatronics Engineering

Here are the main research and projects that I was part of during my Master's in Mechatronics Engineering years:

  • Improved the design of the rehabilitation robots in terms of weight, unique mechanism for the third joint, ease of use, comfort and control by proposing a 3-DOF Shoulder Rehabilitation Robot and implementing Sliding Mode, Lyapunov-based, Inverse Dynamics and Neural Network-based Asymptotic Tracking controllers (Mechanical design in SolidWorks and Controller design in Matlab & Simulink)
  • Successfully eliminated the hand tremor of a Parkinson disease sufferer by designing and building a Hand Tremor Suppression Rehabilitation device (Accelerometers, EMG electrodes and Arduino are utilized, signals are processed using MATLAB)
  • Designed and tested a motor driver to run the 6 DC motors of the 6-DOF Surgical Robot (Altium Designer is used)
  • Studied the locomotion of the ants by designing and building a six-legged ant robot (SolidWorkds, LabVIEW, Proteus and Arduino are utilized)
  • Successfully completed a rescue task by designing and controlling a Shrimp Rover robot (SolidWorks, Proteus and Microcontroller are utilized)
  • Designed and built a Segway (Two-Wheeled) Robot
